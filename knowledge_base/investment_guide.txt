Real Estate Investment Guidelines for King County

BUY signals: Property priced below predicted value, located in high-appreciation zip codes like 98004 98005 98006 98039 98040, good condition rating of 4 or 5, recently renovated.

HOLD signals: Property at fair market value, stable neighborhood, consistent rental demand, no immediate catalysts for major price changes.

AVOID signals: Property significantly overpriced vs prediction, poor condition rating of 1 or 2, zip codes with declining population or employment.

Risk factors to consider:
- Market risk: Interest rate changes affect property demand significantly
- Location risk: Proximity to employment centers drives long-term value
- Condition risk: Properties needing major renovation require capital budget
- Liquidity risk: Real estate takes 30-90 days to sell, not liquid like stocks

ROI calculation: Annual rental income minus expenses divided by purchase price gives rental yield.
Typical rental yields in Seattle area range from 3 to 5 percent annually.
Capital appreciation historically adds another 5 to 8 percent annually in prime areas.
Total return potential is 8 to 13 percent in favorable conditions.

Waterfront homes are luxury assets with limited supply, always hold value well.
Properties built after 2000 have lower maintenance costs than older stock.
Renovated properties reduce near-term capital expenditure risk.
